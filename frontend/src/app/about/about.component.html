Instructions

The goal of this platform is to validate and complement the information under the Current Annotation panel by looking at the data presented in the main section. Then, you will have to fill the form uder the ARG Annotation section.


1. Get familiar with the environment

In order to understand the structure of this website, please, click on the Tour button.

2. New user

If this is the first time you enter the website, you are required to perform two classifications. To do so, click the New User button and perform two ARG annotations. An score with your performance will be prompted. All fields are required, values unrelated to the gene are not accepted.

3. How to get a reward

Once you have done with the training, you are ready to start. First, click on Priority ARGs button and perform one annotation. After you done with the annotation you will get a token number. Please, copy and paste this token to the Amazon Mturk website and submit the form. If you plan to make another Hit DO NOT close the ARG-miner website. Other case you will have to do the training again.

4. Perform a new Hit

First, open a new hit in Amazon Mturk, then, get back to this webpage and just click on the Other ARG Group button. Perform the annotation and submit the token to Amazon Mturk.

Please Be Aware!

If you don't do the mandatory training, the system will not prompt the token.

If the annotation contains random, unrelated or nonsense words, the HIT will be rejected.

Only tokens are accepted, if you submit other values such as gene_id, the HIT will be rejected. Several workers are working in the same gene_id, if the token is not provided, we cannot check your performance.

Micro-Tasks

ARG identification

Please fill up the Antibiotic resistance class, group and mechanism (if any) of the gene based on the observation from the different resources.

The Blast alignment sections associate the queried gene to its closest antibiotic resistance gene from curated databases. From this section you need to look at the scores (Bitscore, evalue, identity and coverage). High identity and coverage represent close related sequences. Statistically significant alignments don't necesarly need high identity percentages. Therefore, pay attention to the Bitscores and Evalues. Note: A bitscore greater than 50 is considered significant if it spans at least 90% coverage.

Mobile Genetic Elements

Horizontal Gene Transfer

Please check if there is any evidence that suggests the gene has been observed in a plasmid, prophage or virus.

To complete this task please check the Plasmid, Virus, or Prophages sections. If there is not any section with plasmid, prophage or virus tags it means that there is not evidence that the gene is transfered by any of those mechanisms.

Pathogen Genomes

Please take a look at the pathogen genomes panel under the horizontal gene transfer section.

This panel will show you the association of the queried gene against a set of ~100,000 bacterial genomes. You will get information about the association of the gene to pathogens (as percentages) as well as their antimicrobial phenotype (resistant, susceptible, intermediate). Rate your findigns according to the provided evidence.

Rate your expertise

Please in a scale from 1 to 5 rate your confidence or expertice level, where 1 depicts a non expert evaluator, and 5 if you have experience working with Antibiotic Resistance Genes.

Rate your annotation confidence

Please in a scale from 1 to 5, rate your confidence in the curation of the queried gene where 1 means that there is a lack of evidence about the gene and 5 the data correlates with your observations.

MGEs and pathogens

Mobile Genetic Elements or MGEs are segments that encode proteins that mediates the movment of DNA within genomes. Plasmids are collection of stable funcional genetic elements. Plasmids can often be transferred to another cell. Bacteriophages can also transfer genetic material by accidentally package segments of DNA to inject into their host.

To identify if the ARG is carried by plasmids, phages or viruses, the gene was aligned to the ACLAME database. Scores provide insight into the presence of this gene into the MGE.

Pathogens are invaders that attack their hosts. The most familiar are viruses and bacteria and they can cause a wide range of infections and diseases. To identify if the gene is hosted in a pathogen genome, the gene was matched to the PATRIC database. Then, we were able to identify the number of pathogen genomes that carry the gene.

Each color represents a different scoring scale

Similarity to known ARGs

To curate a gene as an ARG, it is necesary to validate its evidence. One way to do it is by looking at the information provided from curated databases such as the CARD database. Thus, the entry was compared against the databases to reinforce, reject or modify the current annotation.

For this comparison, the gene was aligned to the genes from the databases using blast and its best hit was extracted. For each database, a panel shows the quality of the alignment and the information regarding that best hit.

Tip To identify the class of antibiotic consider looking at middle to high bitscore, evalue and coverage. However, to identify antibiotic resistance names , we would recomend to look at the panels where the sequence similarity is high with a very strong coverage.
